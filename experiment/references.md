## Textbooks and Books

1. **S.M. Sze and Kwok K. Ng**, *Physics of Semiconductor Devices*, 3rd Edition, Wiley-Interscience, 2006.
   - ISBN: 978-0-471-14323-9
   - Chapter 3: Metal-Semiconductor Contacts (pp. 134-196)
   - Sections 3.1-3.4: Schottky barriers, barrier height, current transport
   - [Wiley Online Library](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Physics+of+Semiconductor+Devices%2C+3rd+Edition-p-9780471143239)

2. **S.M. Sze**, *Semiconductor Devices: Physics and Technology*, 3rd Edition, John Wiley & Sons, 2012.
   - ISBN: 978-0-470-53794-7
   - Chapter 5: Schottky and Ohmic Contacts (pp. 149-175)
   - Sections on rectifying contacts and contact resistance
   - [Wiley Online](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Semiconductor+Devices%3A+Physics+and+Technology%2C+3rd+Edition-p-9780470537947)

3. **Ben G. Streetman and Sanjay Kumar Banerjee**, *Solid State Electronic Devices*, 7th Edition, Pearson, 2014.
   - ISBN: 978-0-133-35614-3
   - Chapter 5: Field Effect Transistors and Related Devices (pp. 180-220)
   - Section 5.1: Metal-Semiconductor Junctions (pp. 180-195)
   - [Pearson](https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/solid-state-electronic-devices/P200000003246)

4. **Robert F. Pierret**, *Semiconductor Device Fundamentals*, Addison-Wesley, 1996.
   - ISBN: 978-0-201-54393-6
   - Chapter 11: Schottky and Ohmic Contacts (pp. 381-421)
   - Detailed derivations of barrier height and I-V characteristics

5. **Donald A. Neamen**, *Semiconductor Physics and Devices: Basic Principles*, 4th Edition, McGraw-Hill, 2011.
   - ISBN: 978-0-073-52958-5
   - Chapter 9: Metal-Semiconductor and Semiconductor Heterojunctions (pp. 373-425)
   - Section 9.1: Schottky Barrier Diode (pp. 373-395)
   - Section 9.2: Ohmic Contacts (pp. 395-405)
   - [McGraw-Hill](https://www.mheducation.com/highered/product/semiconductor-physics-devices-neamen/M9780073529585.html)

6. **E.H. Rhoderick and R.H. Williams**, *Metal-Semiconductor Contacts*, 2nd Edition, Oxford Science Publications, 1988.
   - ISBN: 978-0-198-59336-0
   - Chapters 1-3: Barrier formation, theory, and characteristics (pp. 1-120)
   - Classic reference on metal-semiconductor contact theory
